Scottish Football History






The Scottish Football League was originally founded in 1890 and since then the SPFL(Scottish Premier Football leagues) has grown to 4 leagues with 42 teams playing to a professional level. Rangers have won the most titles at 55 with Celtic in close second at 51 and Aberdeen and Hearts drawing, coming in third with 4 titles each.

Scotland's national team was founded in founded in 1873 making it the second oldest national team in the world. Since then, the team has climbed the world rankings to 39th but has only reached 8 world cups and 3 european championships. Even though Scotland's international record is not as successful as its European neighbours, they have many world class players reaching Champions League Finals and winning leagues such as England's top flight.



Facts about Scottish Football

  • Celtic were the first British club to win the European Cup.
  • Rangers are the most succesful football team in the world with 115 trophies.
  • Dundee and Dundee United are the closest football grounds in Britain, only 200 yards apart.
  • Dundee United are the only team in the world with a 100% win record against FC Barcelona.
  • The highest transfer fee received in the Scottish Professional Football League - Kieran Tierney by Arsenal to Celtic, £25 million.
